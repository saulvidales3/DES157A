Journal Entry #5

Game Design Principles

“Game Design Principles” by juegoadmin is an article that highlights the 7 fundamental design principles to make a successful game. The game should have goals and objectives to keep players motivated and engaged in the game. Second, the mechanics should be simple and easy to understand.

Third, the user interface should be easy to navigate and understand so players can spend time playing instead of trying to figure out how the site works. Fourth, there should be a balance between how challenging and difficult the game is, because if it’s too challenging, players may feel discouraged from playing. Fifth, there should be feedback and rewards to help guide players and keep them motivated.

Sixth playtesting is crucial to make sure the game is interesting and engaging across all stages of the game. Lastly, technical and audio design features make the game more immersive and engaging for users. These principles should create a successful game by keeping users engaged and motivated in the game.

Journal Entry #3

10 Intriguing Photographs to teach Close Reading and Visual Thinking Skills

In the article titled “10 Intriguing Photographs to Teach Close Reading and Visual Thinking Skills” by Michael Gonchar, Gonchar displays 10 photographs by the New York Times that are alluring and mysterious.

This article presents 10 images that can be analyzed and interpreted differently by various viewers. Gonchar highlights the importance of visual thinking skills by sharing some skills students could learn when looking at images. Looking at small details and the overall big picture are important skills needed to fully grasp and understand an image.

Asking questions such as “What is going on?” or “What more can you find?” can help students truly analyze an image and understand every part of the picture. Overall, visual thinking skills are composed of good observations and deep analysis of the overall message being presented.

Journal Entry #2

Best Practices for Modals/Overlays/Dialog

In the reading titled “Best Practices for Modals / Overlays / Dialog Windows” by Naema Baskanderi, I learned that modal windows sit above the main app window to serve as extra guidance for users who may be lost. Modals are used to grab user’s full attention, gather users information, or present extra information to users.

There are a few design principles designers should follow when making modals such as dimming the background when the modal window appears or keeping the modal at the top of the site and not covering the full site. Modals have some accessibility issues in mobile display because modals require the use of a keyboard, which may be problematic for users without access to a laptop. Overall, modals are a great extra piece of guidance in a website to display important information or gather important information from users.

Best Practices for form design

Good Form Design Example

This article is about the best practices in form design and outlines 20 principles designers should follow to keep their designs user-friendly. Web designers could minimize input fields, use a single-column layout, and break long forms into steps to keep users engaged and interested.

Another strategy for having good UI/UX design is to label things carefully and clearly, as well as keyboard navigation, to fill out forms more efficiently. This article mainly highlights the importance of being clear, concise, and inclusive to create a user-friendly design.

Overall, there were many tips on how to keep a web design accessible and easy to navigate to create a website that is easy to use, accessible, and engaging.
