Something my group and I discussed was how internet users' privacy is decreasing and how that will continue in the future if regulations aren't put into action. Our research question was "How will technology respond to privacy regulations."

Although I like to think that technology in the future will respect privacy regulations and policies it seems like this is highly unlikely since many forms of internet media find loopholes around these regulations. Currently, the United States is trying to pass a privacy law however that will take years from now to pass.

I think it's great that the United States is actively trying to pass laws to protect it's citizens’ privacy however I feel like regardless of these laws and regulations being passed the internet will find a way to violate users’ privacy.

Although the internet does actively try to invade users’ privacy, sometimes users make it too easy by consenting to it when agreeing to terms and conditions they don't read or accepting cookies when logging into different websites. I also sometimes do this because I feel overwhelmed by the blocks of text displayed in the terms and conditions or don't have time to go through each piece of text. When I do agree to certain terms and conditions or accept cookies I receive more personalized content which I enjoy from time to time but then find strange when it gets too personal and feel like my privacy is being violated. Overall I like that the United States is trying to protect its citizens’ privacy however I feel like technology will find ways to get around those laws and invade users' privacy.
