Project Overview

My project reimagines traditional city design and dives deep into a city that is built around plants and nature instead of on top of it. My project sort of looks more into a utopian society where plants are prioritized, and cities are completely green.

Overall, this project challenges traditional ways of thinking when approaching city design and tries to convey what if cities were designed around greenery. This project does this through a 3D interactive city and architectural illustrations.

Project Research

For research, I looked into the greenest cities currently in the world and realized that when they meant green, they weren’t being literal like I was. The list of green cities on these sites focused more on clean and renewable energy rather than just an abundance of greenery as I imagined for my city redesign.

I was mainly inspired by nature and the wilderness. I wanted to reimagine a city where all plants were protected under law in the city design process.

To help me design my project, I looked into Google Maps 360 because I wanted to make a 3D interactive city that users could walk through. I also looked into architecture firms’ project portfolios to see how they displayed their architectural drawings because I was planning on doing a handful of these.

Visual Design

For the visual design, I was mainly taking inspiration from architecture portfolios to display my architectural drawings. I also wanted to make the overall aesthetic of my project to match my theme, so that’s why I decided to pursue a green and beige color palette.

User Interface

For my interface, I decided to keep it simple and easy for users to navigate. Overall, I feel like it’s pretty understandable to navigate. Some key interface features in my project may include the interactive city and hovering labels.

Users could look through the 3D interactive city through the arrow keys and zoom into the city by clicking the icon on the lower right side. I wanted users to have the chance to see the 3D city at a bigger scale that takes up the whole screen, as well as be able to escape it by pressing “Esc” to return to the whole project. An essential resource used for my 3D city was the a-frame.io, which helped me compose the green 3D interactive city.

I decided at the last minute to add the hovering labels to make my project more interesting and interactive because it felt like users were just scrolling by and not really interacting. I also like that I took this approach because I cut down a lot of text I was planning on implementing as mini-paragraphs on the side, which may get boring.

Revisions

I did get some good feedback when doing the user testing assignment in class because I got to see firsthand how users may interact with my site. Overall, my classmates who tested out my website understood the message my project was conveying, but they did have some helpful feedback. .

Both Vicky and Amber suggested I make it clearer that the 3D city I had in my project is an interactive piece of my project by adding instructions to click on the arrow keys. They also had me thinking about the block of text I was planning on adding with each illustration. After thinking about it, I asked if hover labels would be better, and they agreed because it would make the project more interesting and interactive.

Overall, the usability test really helped improve my project since I now have a more user-friendly project that is cohesive and easy to use.

Summary

From this capstone project, I have learned that a lot of trial and error goes into design due to the multiple iterations and different ideas I had for my project.

Initially, I wanted to create the perfect city, but then I realized that it is practically impossible since it doesn’t even currently exist. I had to think smaller and focus on certain pieces of the city, and then I landed on designing a radically green city.
